Question 16.2023:   Let X be a random variable with probability density function
$f(x) =
\begin{cases}
    \alpha\lambda x^{\alpha-1}e^{-\lambda x^{\alpha}} & \text{if } x > 0 \\
    0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}$
If the median of $X$ is 1 and the third quantile is 2 then $\brak{\alpha, \lambda}$ equals \\
\solution 
If median of random variable X is 1, then CDF of X evaluated at 1 is equal to $\frac{1}{2}$ \\
The third quantile is defined as \\
\begin{align}
\int_{0}^{Q3}f(x)dx &= \frac{3}{4} \\
Given ,F(1) &= \frac{1}{2} \\
F(2) &= \frac{3}{4} \\
F(x) &= \int_{-\infty}^{x} f(x) dx  \\
x \leq 0 ,  F(x) &= 0 \\
F(x) &= \alpha \lambda \int_{0}^{x} x^{\alpha-1}e^{-\lambda x^{\alpha}}dx  \\
let,   x^{\alpha} &= y 
\end{align}
differentiate both sides 
\begin{align}
\alpha x^{\alpha-1}dx &= dy \\
F(x)  &= \lambda \int e^{-\lambda y} dy \\
      &= -e^{-\lambda y}\\
F(x)  &= \brak{-e^{-\lambda x^{\alpha}}}_{0}^{x}\\
F(x)  &=  1 - e^{-\lambda x^{\alpha}} \\
F(1)  &= \frac{1}{2} \\
\Rightarrow \frac{1}{2} &= 1 - e^{-\lambda}  \\
\lambda &= \ln 2 \\
F(2)  &= \frac{3}{4} \\
\Rightarrow \frac{3}{4} &= 1-e^{-\lambda 2^{\alpha}} \\
\alpha &= 1 
\end{align}
$\therefore \lambda = \ln2$ and $\alpha = 1$
